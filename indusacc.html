Diploma in Professional Industrial Accounting
Course Summary
  • Expertise in Tally to manage the company's financial activities, including finalization of accounts, statutory compliance, and coordination with auditors.
  • Handle all aspects of accounting, tax filing, and audit-related tasks.
Key Course Content
  • Manage accounting in Tally, including finalization of personal, company, and LLP accounts.
  • Handle online payments of statutory dues and e-filing of TDS and GST returns.
  • Perform bank reconciliations and support internal audits.
  • Prepare income tax returns and coordinate with auditors for scrutiny matters.
  • Work closely with CAs and auditors to ensure compliance.
Requirements
  • Proficiency in Tally and accounting practices
  • Strong communication and coordination-skills
Role & Responsibilities
  • Prepare and maintain accurate financial statements and reports, including balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow statements.
  • Monitor and analyze manufacturing costs, identifying areas for cost reduction and efficiency improvements.
  • Conduct monthly, quarterly, and annual account reconciliations and resolve discrepancies.
  • Support the budgeting process by providing relevant data and forecasts related to production costs and expenses.
  • Collaborate with production managers to ensure accurate inventory valuation and control.
  • Assist in the preparation of financial analyses to support strategic decision-making.
  • Ensure compliance with financial regulations and standards, including GAAP and industry-specific guidelines.
  • Manage accounts payable and receivable, ensuring timely processing and reconciliation.
  • Participate in audits and assist with tax preparation and compliance.
  • Stay updated on industry trends and best practices in manufacturing finance.
  • Excellent communication skills in spoken and written English.
